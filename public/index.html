Mehndi
Mehndi marks the beginning of Mudra's wedding festivities. Fresh green henna is applied in intricate bridal patterns on the bride's hands and feet, while family and friends celebrate with music, dance and laughter. The darker the mehndi, the deeper the love — as the saying goes in Gujarati tradition.

🌺
Welcome & Decoration Setup

Floral rangoli at the entrance

Bridal seat decoration with flowers & fabric

Full bridal henna — both hands up to elbows

03
Day Three
Monday · 2 February 2027
The Grand Wedding Day
Morning rituals · Sacred vows · Night wedding ceremony
5:30 AM
🌙
Manglik Muhurat Puja — Before Sunrise
The most auspicious wedding muhurats in Hindu tradition often fall before sunrise. The family pandit performs the opening prayers and the bride is prepared with sacred herbs and ritual water.
Pre-dawn manglik prayers with family priest
Sacred herb bath (ubtan) for the bride
Mother applies first kajal and blesses the bride
Wearing of the chooda (red bangles) ceremony
7:00 AM
🐘
Ganesh Puja & Kanya Puja
Morning begins with prayers to Lord Ganesha to bless the day and remove all obstacles. This is followed by the Kanya Puja — a deeply touching ritual where the bride is worshipped as a form of Goddess Lakshmi before she leaves her parents' home.
Ganesh sthapana and 16-step worship
Kanya puja — bride seated on paat, feet washed
Father applies tilak to bride — emotional moment
Mother feeds bride sweet rice (kheer) with love
All family women sing mangal geet for the bride
8:30 AM
🏠
Grah Shanti & Navgraha Puja
A powerful Vedic ritual to pacify all nine planets and seek their blessings for a harmonious married life. The sacred havan is performed with specific offerings for each planet ensuring the couple's future is filled with prosperity and peace.
Navagraha mandal with 9 sacred colours
Vedic mantras chanted for each planet
Havan kund lit with mango wood and ghee
Special prayers for Shani and Rahu
Both bride and groom's families participate
Pandit performs final aarti and ashirvad
11:00 AM
💇‍♀️
Bridal Getting Ready
The bride enters her transformation. Professional bridal makeup artists, hairstylists and jewellery dressers work together to create Mudra's dream bridal look — elegant, radiant and unforgettable.
Professional bridal makeup — 3 to 4 hours
Bridal hair — bun, braid or open style with flowers
Draping of bridal lehenga or saree by mother
Jewellery dressing — nath, mang tikka, necklace, bangles
Bridal reveal moment — first look for the family
11:00 PM
☀️
Afternoon Puja — Ashirwad Ceremony
All relatives and family elders are gathered for a special afternoon puja and ashirvad ceremony where every elder personally blesses Mudra before her wedding. A deeply emotional and meaningful ritual.
Bride touches feet of every elder present
Elders give cash shagun and blessings
Collective prayer for bride's happiness
Family group photographs with the bride
Emotional farewell preparations begin
6:30 PM
🎺
Baraat Arrival — Groom's Procession
The groom's baraat arrives at PG Banquet Hall with full fanfare — dhol, shehnai, fireworks and a dancing procession. The bride's family receives the baraat with aarti, flowers and a warm welcome.
Groom arrives on decorated horse / car
Live dhol-tasha band leads the baraat
Friends and family dance in the procession
Bride's mother performs groom's aarti at entrance
Jaimala — garland exchange between the couple
📍 PG Banquet Hall Entrance
8:00 PM
🔥
Vivah Vidhi — Sacred Wedding Ceremony
The most sacred and eternal moment — the Vedic wedding ceremony. As the sacred fire (Agni) bears witness, ancient Sanskrit mantras are chanted by the priest and the couple takes their lifelong vows.
Kanyadaan — father gives away his daughter with love
Hasta Milap — joining of hands, tied with sacred thread
Mangalsutra — groom ties the sacred necklace
Sindoor ceremony — vermilion applied
Saptapadi — 7 sacred vows around the holy fire
Dhruva Darshan — viewing the North Star together
Ashirvad from all elders of both families
📍 Main Mandap · PG Banquet Hall
8:00 PM
🍽️
Grand Wedding Reception Dinner
A spectacular wedding dinner reception at PG Banquet Hall. Guests are treated to a royal spread while the newlyweds receive blessings and take photographs with everyone present.
Newlyweds welcomed to stage with flower shower
Grand buffet — 50+ dishes, live counters
Special couple's first dinner served at the stage
Cake cutting ceremony with family
Photography with all guests on stage
DJ music and final dance celebration
Dessert counter — ice cream, mithai, pastry
Night Reception
📍 PG Banquet Hall
12:00 AM
🥲
Vidaai — A Bittersweet Farewell
The most emotional moment of any wedding — the Vidaai. Mudra bids farewell to her family, her childhood home and everything she has known, beginning a beautiful new journey with her husband. Tears, hugs, blessings and immense love fill the air.
Bride throws back handfuls of rice — blessings for family
Brother carries the bride to the car (Govdhana ritual)
Final embrace with mother, father and siblings
Flower shower as the couple departs
Decorated bridal car send-off
